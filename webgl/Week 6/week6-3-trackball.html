A virtual Track ball.Use the mouse position to control rotation about axes. We assume that the ball has a radius 1 unit. We can map a position on its surface to the plane y = 0 by doing an orthogonal projection to the plane (x, 0, z). The three dimensional point that is projected to the point on the plane must satisfy the equation of the sphere x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1.

Suppose that we have two positions oon the hemisphere p1, p2; then the vectors from the origin to these points determine the orientation of the plane, whose normal is n = P1 X P2. The metion of the trackball that moves from P1 to P2 can be achieved by a rotation around n. P1 and P2 have uniot length, then |sin(theta)| = |n|. For small thetas , sin(theta) ~= theta.
